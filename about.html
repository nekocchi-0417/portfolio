About me

Back in university, I studied how people think, relate, and behave - through the lenses of psychology and sociology.

This academic curiosity soon drew me to try different stints, from research to UX, to service design and behavioural insights. My path has always been anchored by a key question:

"How might we design not just for people, but with a deep understanding of them?"

Today, I enjoy zooming into problems to untangle usability frictions through behavioural science and user research, and zooming out to reimagine systems and services at scale. I am most energised when solving complex challenges where human needs, organisational constraints, policy/service ambitions, and creative problem-solving collide.

1. Visual Rebranding for Asia's leading Human-Computer Interaction Lab

As one of the most active HCI research centres in Asia, the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Lab at the National University of Singapore (NUS) sought to strengthen its branding and visibility — beginning with a logo revamp. I took on this challenge as their design intern in 2020.

To inform the redesign, I worked closely with lab members by conducting surveys and interviews to understand their vision, values, and how they perceived the lab culture. The final logo was developed through multiple rounds of iteration and feedback, ensuring it reflected both the lab's identity and aspirations.

To enhance the lab's branding, I was also involved in designing the lab's promotional video and newsletters, together with other design interns (see below).

2. Project Relive x Relove

This was a campus-wide environmental campaign aimed at promoting sustainability through creative engagement, launched in 2021.

As the project lead, I assembled a team of five, and secured $2,200 in funding from the university to launch the 3-month campaign.

The initiative featured exhibitions, upcycling workshops, and social media outreach, and brought together 9 wonderful collaborators — including eco-enterprises, social start-ups, NIE art students, and student groups involved in global environmental movements.

I also led the campaign's visual design, including crafting the logo, posters, social media content, and exhibit visuals.

3. Logo design for Uncommon Minds

Uncommon Minds is a non-profit organisation that hopes to celebrate the uniqueness of neurodiverse individuals, such as individuals with ADHD, dyslexia, and autism through sharing their stories and voices. I worked with the organisation to revamp their logo and visual kit in 2020.
